# Legal, Regulatory, and Intellectual Property

## Exam Relevance
This topic is critical for Domain 1. The CISSP exam expects you to understand the different types of laws and how they apply to security management. You must distinguish between different forms of Intellectual Property (IP) and know the major regulatory requirements (HIPAA, SOX, GLBA, GDPR/CCPA).

## Key Concepts
- **Criminal vs. Civil vs. Administrative**: Criminal is against society (prison), Civil is between parties (money), Administrative is agency-led (compliance).
- **Intellectual Property (IP)**:
  - **Patents**: 20 years for utility; requires disclosure.
  - **Copyright**: Life + 70 years; expression of ideas.
  - **Trademarks**: Brands/logos.
  - **Trade Secrets**: Must be kept secret; no expiration as long as secret.
- **Regulations**:
  - **HIPAA**: Healthcare (PHI).
  - **SOX**: Public company financial records.
  - **GLBA**: Financial institutions (PII).
  - **CCPA/GDPR**: Privacy rights for individuals.

## Exam Traps
- **Patent vs. Trade Secret**: If the question mentions "not wanting to disclose," it's a Trade Secret. If it mentions "monopoly for 20 years," it's a Patent.
- **Copyright and Software**: Software code is protected by Copyright (as an expression), though specific algorithms might be patented.
- **Jurisdiction**: Be aware of the difference between EU (GDPR) and US (sector-specific) privacy approaches.
