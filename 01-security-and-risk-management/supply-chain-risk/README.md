# Supply Chain Risk Management (SCRM)

## Exam Relevance
Supply Chain Risk Management has become a major focus area in the CISSP Domain 1. As organizations rely more on third-party cloud services, SaaS, and complex hardware/software components, the ability to manage these external risks is vital.

## Key Concepts
- **Vendor Lifecycle**: Selection, Onboarding, Monitoring, Renewal/Termination. Security must be integrated at every stage.
- **Contractual Protections**:
  - **SLA**: Metrics and penalties.
  - **MSA**: General framework terms.
  - **Right to Audit**: Ability to verify security.
- **SCRM Transparency**:
  - **SBOM**: Knowing what's inside your software.
  - **Hardware Integrity**: Mitigating counterfeit parts.
- **Reporting**:
  - **SOC 1**: Financial controls.
  - **SOC 2 Type I**: Point-in-time security.
  - **SOC 2 Type II**: Security over a period of time (more valuable).

## Exam Traps
- **SOC 1 vs SOC 2**: If the question is about "financial statement accuracy," choose SOC 1. If it's about "security/privacy," choose SOC 2.
- **Upstream vs Downstream**: Remember that "Upstream" is towards your suppliers, and "Downstream" is towards your customers.
- **SLA vs MSA**: The SLA is about *performance*; the MSA is about the *legal relationship*.
