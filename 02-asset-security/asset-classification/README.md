# Asset Classification and Management (Domain 2.1)

This section covers the identification, valuation, and classification of information and assets.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Asset Valuation
* **Tangible Assets**: Physical items (servers, buildings).
* **Intangible Assets**: Intellectual property, reputation, brand equity.
* **Valuation Metrics**: Replacement cost, historical cost, consequential loss.

### 2. Information Classification
* **Government/Military Levels**:
    * **Top Secret**: Exceptionally grave damage.
    * **Secret**: Serious damage.
    * **Confidential**: Damage.
    * **Unclassified**: No damage.
* **Commercial/Private Levels**:
    * **Highly Confidential / Restricted**: Trade secrets, high impact.
    * **Private / Confidential**: PII, internal HR data.
    * **Internal Use Only**: Non-public but low impact.
    * **Public**: Marketing materials.

### 3. Roles and Responsibilities
* **Data Owner (Accountable)**: Senior management. Determines classification and retention.
* **Data Custodian (Responsible)**: IT/Admins. Implements controls, backups, and ACLs.
* **Data Steward**: Focuses on data quality and metadata standards.

### 4. Inventory and Management
* **CMDB**: Configuration Management Database.
* **SAM**: Software Asset Management (licenses, versions).
* **Shadow IT**: Unmanaged assets/software used without approval.

## Study Tips
* Remember that the **Data Owner** is always ultimately accountable.
* Understand the **NIST SP 800-88** first step: **Categorize** the information.
* Use the **High-Water Mark** principle for aggregate systems.
